Phase 3

Content Strategy · Terrance McMahon

Prepared 2026-05-12 by AG1 Consulting. Distribution and content pillars built around the new four-tier ladder, the Algorithm-to-Recreate-Yourself methodology, and the LinkedIn corporate-keynote opening.

Strategic Frame

The goal is not "more content." Terrance's audience is already enormous (1M+ cross-platform, ~169K on IG alone). The goal is content that compounds the new four-tier ladder (books, app, mentorship, speaking) and opens the corporate-keynote lane that the credential stack already supports but the offer architecture does not currently surface.

Three pillars. Five channels. One repurpose pipeline driven from a single weekly long-form session.

Pillar 1 · The Algorithm (Method, Not Motivation)

Audience served: the existing 1M+ cross-platform audience plus search-arriving readers who land on terrancemcmahon.com from the TEDx talk or from book buyers.

Why it compounds the ladder: prospects who understand the algorithm as a structured four-move method (not a vague motivational frame) are the ones who buy the books, subscribe to the app, and apply for mentorship. Method-led content pre-frames the deeper containers.

Content shapes:

  • The four moves of the algorithm, broken into individual long-form pieces
  • The TEDx talk, repurposed: 6-8 individual posts pulling the structural beats forward
  • Pattern recognition (without naming individuals): the patterns Terrance has seen across the Re-Create cohorts
  • Failure modes (why most "re-create yourself" attempts stall, what the structural fix is)
  • Book chapter excerpts, structurally surfaced

Voice rules: bold, methodical, story-anchored. No motivational-poster register. AG1 universal banned-phrase list applies, see copy-strategy. The register is operator-mentor, faith-aware, redemption-anchored.

Pillar 2 · The Arc (Testimony As Through-Line)

Audience served: the faith-aware audience, the rebuilding-after-setback audience, the Christian-business cohort that responds to the literal redemption arc.

Why it compounds the ladder: every post that anchors the redemption arc (350lb plus 90 days to live plus liver from a young prisoner plus MassMutual seat) re-anchors why the algorithm has weight. The arc is the warranty on the methodology. By the time the audience encounters the ladder, the algorithm reads as the export of the story, not as a content product.

Content shapes:

  • The eight-year anniversary of the transplant (annual marker post)
  • The before-and-after of the literal arc (carefully, with specificity, never as performance)
  • The young prisoner in Alabama (the specific dignity of the source of the liver)
  • The MassMutual seat (the operator credential, surfaced in context)
  • The first year after transplant (the pivot story, broken into chapters)

Voice rules: specific, grounded, never performative. The arc is the proof. Avoid retelling-as-marketing. Tell the story when it serves the methodology, not when it serves the engagement.

Pillar 3 · The Bible, Business, And AI (The Signature Frame)

Audience served: executives, founders, Christian-business operators, and the corporate-keynote buyers who book from the LinkedIn surface. This is the LinkedIn audience plus the X long-form audience, not the IG carousel audience.

Why it compounds the ladder: corporate keynote contracts ($10K-$50K per engagement) are an entire revenue lane that the current architecture under-routes. The audience signal is already there (the credential stack is built). What is missing is the cadence at the executive register that pulls B2B inbound.

Content shapes:

  • The high-performance case for the algorithm (not the wellness case, the operator case)
  • The leadership-team case (executive offsite, all-hands, leadership-retreat applications)
  • The faith-aware business case (the "Bible, Business and AI" frame, surfaced in long-form)
  • Case-pattern posts (without naming clients): the operator failure modes the algorithm catches
  • The AI lane: practical AI applications inside a Christian-business operating frame

Voice rules: bold, structured, executive-fluent. Faith register is welcome but never preachy. The audience reads LinkedIn essays plus X long-form threads, not IG carousels. Long-form. Methodical. Driving.

Channel Allocation

Instagram (@terrance.mcmahon · ~169K)

  • Cadence: 5-6 posts per week (Reel-led)
  • Mix: 50% Pillar 1 (algorithm), 30% Pillar 2 (arc), 20% Pillar 3 (Bible, Business, AI signature)
  • Function: brand depth, audience compounding, ladder pre-frame
  • Stories: daily, light, behind-the-build, Re-Create app activity, book promotion, podcast appearances, keynote calendar

TikTok

  • Cadence: 3-5 per week (clip-down from IG Reels plus original)
  • Mix: 70% Pillar 1, 20% Pillar 2, 10% Pillar 3
  • Function: cross-platform compounding. Less B2B routing, more brand depth and ladder pre-frame.

YouTube

  • Cadence: 1 long-form per week (12-25 min target length)
  • Mix: 50% Pillar 1 (algorithm long-form, methodology build-out), 30% Pillar 2 (arc-anchored teaching), 20% Pillar 3 (executive crossover)
  • Function: buyer-readiness channel. The place where prospects research before they commit to the app, mentorship, or a corporate booking.
  • Repurpose: every long-form clips into 3-5 short-form pieces for IG Reels, Threads, X.

LinkedIn (new cadence at the executive register)

  • Cadence: 3-4 long-form essays per week
  • Mix: 60% Pillar 3 (Bible-Business-AI plus corporate-keynote positioning), 30% Pillar 1 (algorithm at the executive register), 10% Pillar 2 (arc, at the operator register)
  • Function: corporate-keynote inbound. Build the LinkedIn footprint that pulls $10K-$50K engagements from HR, leadership teams, conference programmers, and faith-business communities.
  • Posting style: long-form essay format. Carousel deck for the higher-leverage methodology pieces.

X / Twitter

  • Cadence: 1 long-form thread per week plus 5-7 short posts
  • Mix: 40% Pillar 1 (algorithm, X-fluent register), 30% Pillar 3 (Bible-Business-AI signature), 30% Pillar 2 (arc)
  • Function: Christian-business cohort capture, freedom-builder cohort capture. The faith-aware business cohort lives here at high concentration and is comfortable with explicit framing.

Threads

  • Cadence: 1-2 posts per day
  • Mix: repurposed lines from the long-form, observational notes, audience-conversation pieces
  • Function: brand maintenance, audience interaction, low-friction daily presence

Repurpose Pipeline

One YouTube long-form per week feeds:

  • 3-5 IG Reels (vertical clips with subtitle burn-in)
  • 3-5 TikTok clips (parallel)
  • 1 LinkedIn essay (textual rewrite at the executive register)
  • 1 X long-form thread (line-by-line condensation)
  • 4-6 Threads posts (individual lines lifted from the long-form)
  • 1 podcast clip repurposed if a relevant clip exists from a recent appearance

One recording session per week. Six surfaces fed. Buildable without a team if the writer-editor handles the repurpose pass and Terrance handles the recording.

90-Day Roadmap

Days 1-30 · Build The Distribution Foundation

  • LinkedIn cadence launches at 3x per week (executive register)
  • X long-form thread cadence launches at 1x per week
  • YouTube long-form cadence launches at 1x per week (Pillar 1 anchored)
  • Repurpose pipeline live by Day 14
  • First corporate-keynote inbound conversation expected by Day 21-30

Days 31-60 · Compound + Pre-Frame The Mentorship Cohort

  • LinkedIn to 4x per week
  • YouTube to 1-2x per week if production capacity allows
  • Private mentorship cohort opens in Week 8 (after 6 weeks of Pillar 1 + Pillar 3 pre-framing)
  • First mentorship applications expected in Week 8-10

Days 61-90 · First Mentorship Cohort Closes + First Corporate Keynote Booked

  • First private mentorship cohort closes mid-Week 10
  • First corporate-keynote engagement booked
  • LinkedIn DM rate from verified executive / HR / event-producer profiles measurable
  • Iterate the content mix based on which pillar is driving the most ladder conversion

Voice Calibration Across Pillars

The voice register stays consistent across all three pillars: bold + redemption-arc-anchored + algorithm-framed. What shifts is vocabulary and structural cadence:

  • Pillar 1 vocabulary: algorithm, methodology, four moves, build-out, run the move, the practice, the protocol
  • Pillar 2 vocabulary: the arc, the transplant, the year after, the seat, the pivot, the work in between
  • Pillar 3 vocabulary: leadership, decision-making, the operating system, the executive case, the corporate frame, the offsite

Across all three, the forbidden vocabulary is consistent: AG1 universal banned-phrase list applies, see copy-strategy. Three registers to cut on sight: hustle-bro, new-age / pop-spiritual, AI-slop. Plus: no urgency-fabrication ("limited spots" only when literally true), no Bible-verse-as-proof in conversion copy (the faith register is signature, not sermon).

Metrics That Matter

Not vanity. Three numbers per pillar.

Pillar 1 (Algorithm): - Save rate on IG (proxy for "this is reference material, I'll come back to it") - Watch-time on YouTube long-form (proxy for buyer-readiness depth) - Free-chapter download rate from cold YouTube traffic

Pillar 2 (Arc): - Comments-per-post on arc-anchored content (proxy for "your story landed") - DM rate after the annual transplant-anniversary post - Book sales velocity tied to arc-pillar releases

Pillar 3 (Bible, Business, AI): - LinkedIn essay impressions (proxy for executive-audience reach) - DM rate from verified executive / HR / event-producer profiles - Corporate-keynote inbound rate (target: 1-2 qualified inbound per month by Day 60)

What Gets Cut

The 1M+ cross-platform audience does not need more aesthetic-only content. The audience needs more algorithm-led methodology content (Pillar 1), more specific-arc-anchored content (Pillar 2), and (new) executive-register Bible-Business-AI content (Pillar 3) on LinkedIn and X.

The content that gets cut:

  • Generic motivation posts
  • Quote-card-only posts without methodology context
  • Anything in the new-age / pop-spiritual register (clashes with the Bible-explicit signature)
  • Anything that abstracts the redemption arc into metaphor
  • Anything that confuses operator-mentor register with hustle-coach register
  • Bible-verse-as-CTA (the faith register is signature, not sermon)

End of content strategy.
