Two parallel sequences feed the same destination (the new four-tier ladder):

Sequence Notes

Cadence

Days 0, 1, 3, 5, 7, 10, 12, 14. Eight touches across 14 days. The early cluster (Days 0, 1, 3) catches the after-glow window from the book download, the TEDx watch, or the podcast appearance. The middle cluster (Days 5, 7, 10) maps to the structural curve of running the four moves. The late cluster (Days 12, 14) closes with quiet invitations into the broader ladder.

Voice

Bold. Story-led-then-method. TEDx-stage cadence. Each email reads as if Terrance wrote it the morning after a stage rehearsal. Period-heavy cadence, short paragraphs, structural sentences. The arc is referenced specifically (350lb, 90 days, prisoner-from-Alabama) when it serves the methodology. The faith register is signature, not sermon (the "Bible, Business and AI" line appears in the close, not in every email).

CTAs

Every CTA is invitational, not directive. "Link is below if you want to look at it" not "Click here now." The hub URL or the relevant landing page is the only clickable per email, never two competing CTAs.

Subjects

Bold-declarative, method-led, specific. Open rates target above-industry for a TEDx-anchored list (35-55% range on the warmest cohort post-TEDx). Subjects do not bait curiosity.

Branching

The two sequences share Day 12 and Day 14 (the closing pair). Above Day 12, they branch on entry context (book downloader vs TEDx/podcast opt-in).

Cross-channel

These emails should not duplicate the IG content. The cadence is parallel, not redundant. Email is the deeper, slower-cadence channel. IG is the surface channel. LinkedIn essays cover the executive register that the emails do not.

The redemption arc, on cadence

The literal arc (90 days, liver, prisoner-from-Alabama, 350lb) appears in Sequence A on Day 3 and in Sequence B on Day 2, both as the warranty on the methodology. The arc does not appear in every email (which would read as performance). The discipline is "the arc when it serves the methodology," not "the arc as a recurring brand element."

End of email sequences.
