Foundation

Copy Strategy · Terrance McMahon

Prepared 2026-05-12 by AG1 Consulting. Voice rules, headline patterns, framing patterns, and forbidden-vocabulary list. Anchored on the Terrance voice register (bold + redemption-arc-anchored + faith-aware + algorithm-framed).

Voice Register

One sentence

A former MassMutual CEO who almost died, came back, and now writes in TEDx-stage cadence about the algorithm that re-created him, with the weight of someone whose redemption arc is literal (not metaphorical) and whose business arc is documented (not implied).

Three weights it sits between

  • Heavier than coaching-content language. No "level up," no "elevate," no operator-bro hype cadence. The story holds the weight, the copy doesn't have to perform it.
  • Bolder than meditation-teacher language. The voice is action-oriented, the cadence is structured-but-driving. Not contemplative. Re-Create not retreat.
  • Bible-aware without being preachy. The "Bible, Business and AI" framing is a signature, not a sermon. The faith register lives in the bio and the framing, not in every line.

Pace

Structured. TEDx-stage cadence. The reader can hear the cadence of someone who has told this story from a stage and knows where the beats land. Period-heavy, short paragraphs, story-led-then-method.

Stance

Not the expert performing authority. The man whose authority is the arc itself. The story did the credentialing. The methodology is the export of the story.

Headline Patterns

Pattern A · The redemption-arc declarative

"Ninety days to live. Liver from a young prisoner. Three-fifty pounds to healthy. The algorithm came after."

Anchored on the literal arc. The numbers do the work. No metaphor.

Pattern B · The bold reversal

"The version of you that re-creates is not the one who tries harder. It is the one who runs a different algorithm."

Disarms the hustle-coach register without naming it. Reads natural in Terrance's voice because "algorithm" is his actual frame.

Pattern C · The Bible, Business, AI signature

"The Bible, business, and AI. Three frames, one operating system. Re-create runs at the intersection."

The faith-business signature, surfaced as a unifying frame, not as a sermon.

Pattern D · The CEO-to-mentor pivot

"I ran the Northern New England seat at MassMutual. Then I almost died. Then I built a different kind of company. The methodology in between is the part most people skip."

Operator credentialing plus the arc plus the methodology, in three sentences.

Pattern E · The methodology, named (TEDx-anchored)

"From broken to beautiful. The algorithm to re-create yourself. Eighteen minutes of it lives on a TEDx stage. The build-out is what the books, the app, and the work are for."

The TEDx line, surfaced. The methodology has a name. The catalogue is the build-out.

Framing Patterns

The 3-act redemption frame

  1. The "before" (350lb, MassMutual seat, 90 days to live, the literal arc)
  2. The pivot (the liver transplant, the year of rebuilding, the algorithm)
  3. The export (the books, the TEDx, the app, the work)

Used across hero VSL openings, long-form essays, podcast pre-frames.

The "method, not motivation" frame

Resist motivation-content register. Every promise gets anchored to the algorithm.

  • Not: "You can re-create yourself."
  • Yes: "The algorithm has four moves. The TEDx walks through them. The books build them out. The app runs them daily."

The "faith-aware, not preachy" frame

The Bible-explicit framing is a signature, not a sermon.

  • Yes: "The Bible, business, and AI. Three frames, one operating system."
  • No: Bible verse citations as proof in conversion copy.
  • The faith register lives in the bio, the framing, and the audience-self-selection. It does not live in every sentence.

The "literal arc, not metaphorical" frame

The redemption arc is concrete. The copy should resist abstracting it into a metaphor.

  • Yes: "Three-fifty pounds. Ninety days to live. Liver from a young prisoner in Alabama."
  • No: "Hit rock bottom and found my way back."

The specificity is the proof. Without the specificity, this becomes another transformation-coach brand. With the specificity, it reads as testimony.

The "Made You Something" Frame (Outreach Specific)

Light. Peer-to-peer. Gift-frame. Never apology-led, never transaction-led.

The 21-month-old prior thread is referenced offhand, in one sentence, never twice. The structural piece is the new angle (the explicit four-tier ladder across the books, the app, the mentorship, the speaking), not a callback to the burned $100k/mo Loom from August 2024.

Forbidden Vocabulary

AG1 universal banned-phrase list applies. See the AG1 master banned-phrase reference for the full enumeration. Three registers to cut on sight, summarised:

  • Hustle-bro register.
  • New-age / pop-spiritual register (this one matters extra here because the Bible-explicit brand clashes hard against it).
  • AI-slop register.

Triple-adjective lists, "not just X, but Y" constructions, urgency-fabrication, and impersonal voice all get cut as well.

Punctuation rule (lint gate)

  • Zero em dashes. Use commas, periods, or parentheses. The only allowed em-dash-position is a sign-off ("- Andrei") and even there a hyphen-space is the standard.

Cross-Asset Style Notes

Across hub, landing, emails, VSL

  • First-person. Terrance speaks as Terrance. No third-person founder-bio voice on his surfaces.
  • One claim per sentence. Resist stacked claims. The arc plus the methodology has natural weight, the copy does not need to compound it.
  • Specific over scenic. Numbers, dates, places, the prisoner-from-Alabama detail. Not "many," not "transformative," not "life-changing."
  • The methodology has a name. "The algorithm to re-create yourself." Use it consistently. Default to "the algorithm" as the unifying noun once the full name has been introduced.

Across IG (@terrance.mcmahon)

  • Bold. Action-oriented. Faith-aware where it reads natural.
  • Reels-first. The TEDx-stage cadence works as voice-over on Reels.
  • Bible references allowed but not required, and never as the conversion close.

Across LinkedIn

  • Long-form essay format
  • Open with the corporate-credential frame (former MassMutual CEO) when the audience is B2B
  • Close with the methodology, not the CTA
  • Cadence: 4-6 paragraphs, structured-but-driving

Across X / Twitter

  • Long-form threads work well in this register
  • Open with a redemption-arc fragment, then the methodology
  • Faith register is allowed at higher concentration here (X cohort skews comfortable with Bible-explicit voice)

Across YouTube long-form

  • Open in voice, on camera, no B-roll
  • TEDx-stage cadence in production-grade lighting
  • Close with a quiet invitation, not a hard CTA

Outreach-Specific Notes

The "made you something" frame stays light. The August 2024 thread reference is one offhand sentence, never two. The hub link is the only clickable. The new structural angle (the explicit four-tier ladder across the books, the app, mentorship, and speaking) is the lead, the prior $100k/mo dollar-claim is named once and only inside the bridge sentence to acknowledge that the new angle is structurally different from the old one.

No minute-CTA on first touch. No payment terms in any outreach asset. No AG1 service pricing.

Faith-Register Calibration

The "Bible, Business and AI" framing is the brand signature. The copy approach across all surfaces:

  • Bio, headers, framing: faith-explicit is welcome. "The Bible, Business, and AI" reads as a signature line.
  • Body copy: faith-aware, not faith-led. The Christian-business cohort self-selects from the framing, the body does not need to keep selling them.
  • Conversion copy: zero Bible-verse-as-proof. The methodology converts on methodology, not on scripture-as-warrant.
  • The arc: the liver transplant story has natural redemption-arc weight without ever invoking a religious frame. The audience reads the spiritual register into it on their own.

The discipline here is the same as the "method, not motivation" discipline: the faith-register lives in the framing, the methodology converts the readers.

Last Note

The voice should read as if Terrance could have written it himself, the morning after a TEDx-stage rehearsal, with the cadence still settled into the cadence of the talk. The copy is in service of his brand, not in service of AG1's register. AG1 register only surfaces on AG1's own assets (the hub, the credentials block, the install plan). Everywhere else, Terrance's voice carries.

End of copy strategy.
