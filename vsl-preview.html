Phase 1

VSL Preview Script · Terrance McMahon

Prepared 2026-05-12 by AG1 Consulting. Short preview outline for the hero VSL that lives on the consolidated home page (terrancemcmahon.com). Full produced length is 4-7 minutes, the outline below covers the 3-5 minute preview cut that ships on Day 1 of install.

Voice register: bold + redemption-arc-anchored + algorithm-framed. First-person Terrance. TEDx-stage cadence. The redemption arc is concrete (350lb, 90 days, prisoner-from-Alabama, MassMutual seat). The methodology is named (the algorithm to re-create yourself, four moves). The faith register is signature, not sermon.

Production Frame

Camera: single camera, locked off. Terrance center-frame, slight three-quarter angle. The recording can happen in a natural location (his home office, a quiet study, a setting that reads operator-mentor rather than studio-bro) rather than against a green screen or studio backdrop.

Sound: lavalier mic. Natural ambient noise allowed at low level. The recording does not need to be sterile, but the audio needs to carry the TEDx-stage register.

Pace: structured but driving. The pauses are TEDx-pauses, not meditation-pauses. The cadence is the export of someone who has told this story from a stage.

Length: 3-5 min preview cut (Day 1 ship). 4-7 min full produced version replaces the preview in Phase Three.

Beats

Beat 1 · 0:00-0:30 · The Cold Open (the literal numbers, no metaphor)

Terrance on camera, calm, opens with the literal numbers. Not "Hi, I'm Terrance" and not a hook claim. Opens with the arc, in specifics.

Line:

"Ninety days to live. Three hundred and fifty pounds. A MassMutual seat in Northern New England that did not matter at that point because I was not going to live to keep it.

The liver came at the very end of the window. From a young prisoner in Alabama. The algorithm came after."

Hold the line. Let it land before continuing. The numbers do the work.

Beat 2 · 0:30-1:00 · The Pivot, In One Pass

The eight-year arc, surfaced in compressed form. Not the romantic version, the honest version.

Line:

"The first year after the transplant, I did not have a methodology. I had survival.

The methodology came in years two and three, and only because the survival forced it. I had been given a second seat in a body I had not earned. The question was what to do with it.

That question became the algorithm. The algorithm became the work I do now."

The story is in service of the methodology, not in service of itself. One sentence sets the survival year, one paragraph sets the pivot.

Beat 3 · 1:00-1:45 · The Methodology, Named

The four-move algorithm, surfaced. Not in marketing language, in teaching language.

Line:

"What I teach across every container I run is the same four-move algorithm. The TEDx talk walks through them in eighteen minutes. The books build them out chapter by chapter. The Re-Create app runs them daily.

Move one is the quiet one. It has to settle before anything else does. Most people skip it because it doesn't look like progress.

Move two follows. Move three is the first visible one. Move four is the export.

The moves are sequential, not parallel. The order is the methodology."

This is the pre-frame for the books, the app, and the work. The methodology has a name. The catalogue is the build-out.

Beat 4 · 1:45-2:30 · The Pattern, Across The Cohorts

The audience-proof at the cohort level, surfaced honestly. Not as a marketing number, as observation.

Line:

"Across the Re-Create app cohorts, the book readers, the keynote audiences, and the eight years of work after the transplant, the patterns repeat.

The one I see most is the person who knows about the algorithm and runs move three instead of move one. The visible move. The motivation-led move. The one that looks like progress on social media.

The fix is not more motivation. The fix is the quiet move that nobody can see.

Move one. Same trigger. Daily. That is the prescription that holds across almost everyone I have seen succeed with the algorithm."

The claim is in observation tense, not present-marketing tense. It reads as data, not as performance.

Beat 5 · 2:30-3:30 · The Ladder, Walked Through Briefly

Four tiers, named once each. The ladder is visible, not pitched.

Line:

"What I built is a four-tier ladder for the algorithm, in order of depth.

The books are the entry. Three of them. Each one walks through a different layer of the four moves. Most people start with the first one.

The Re-Create app is the recurring layer. The four moves, run daily, with structure, with cadence, with a small community of people running the same moves.

The private mentorship cohort is the high-ticket tier. Application-only. For the freedom builders who want the methodology installed at the level where the specific blocks get worked through with me directly.

The speaking and enterprise work is the fourth tier. For the operators, teams, and conferences who want the methodology installed at the leadership level.

All of it is on the page below. You don't have to decide today which one is right. The books are the start. Everything else is for after."

Naming the ladder, not pitching the ladder. The audience makes the choice on their own time.

Beat 6 · 3:30-4:00 · The Signature Frame

One small frame, the brand signature. Faith-aware, not preachy.

Line:

"The Bible, business, and AI. Three frames. One operating system.

Re-create runs at the intersection of all three. The Bible holds the framework. Business holds the math. AI holds the leverage.

The algorithm is the synthesis."

Place + practice + small reveal. The brand signature pre-frames the methodology for the right audience.

Beat 7 · 4:00-4:30 · The Close

Quiet invitation, not directive CTA. The page does the rest.

Line:

"If the algorithm has landed for you, the page below has the whole ladder in one view.

Start where the timing is right. The books are the natural entry. The app is for after. The mentorship and speaking work are for the freedom builders who want the methodology installed at the leadership level.

The arc is the warranty. The algorithm is the work.

See you on the page."

End on the methodology, not on the CTA. The CTA is implicit. The viewer routes to the ladder on their own initiative.

Cutdown Versions (Phase Two Deliverables)

The hero VSL produces three short cutdowns for the program landings:

Cutdown 1 · Free Chapter / Book Landing · 90 seconds

Beat 1 (cold open) + Beat 3 (just the algorithm-named paragraph) + Beat 7 (close).

Cutdown 2 · Re-Create App Landing · 90 seconds

Beat 1 (cold open, compressed) + Beat 4 (the pattern across cohorts) + Beat 5 (just the app paragraph from the ladder) + Beat 7 (close).

Cutdown 3 · Private Mentorship Landing · 2 minutes

Beat 2 (the pivot, in one pass) + Beat 4 (the pattern) + Beat 5 (just the mentorship paragraph) + Beat 6 (signature frame) + Beat 7 (close).

Cutdown 4 · Speaking / Enterprise Landing · 2 minutes (optional)

Beat 1 (cold open, MassMutual-anchored) + Beat 3 (algorithm-named) + Beat 5 (just the speaking paragraph) + Beat 7 (close).

All cutdowns are recorded once during the hero VSL session, edited from the same master file. No second shoot required.

Production Notes

  • The opening 60 seconds is in voice, on camera, no B-roll
  • B-roll inserts allowed after Beat 2 (TEDx archive footage, book covers, Re-Create app interface, keynote stages)
  • No music for the first 90 seconds. Light ambient music can layer in from Beat 4 onward, low volume.
  • Subtitles burned in for the IG / TikTok / YouTube / LinkedIn repurposes
  • Color grade: warm, action-oriented, brand-aligned. The aesthetic matches the orange-and-navy palette of terrancemcmahon.com, not generic-creator register.

What Gets Cut From The Recording

These lines should not appear in the VSL, even if they feel natural during the recording session:

  • Any claim about "$100k/mo" or any dollar-claim about audience monetization
  • Any marketing-claim-register language from the AG1 universal banned-phrase list (see copy-strategy)
  • Any pop-spiritual / new-age framing (clashes hard with the Bible-explicit brand)
  • Any of the AG1 universal banned phrases (see copy-strategy for the full list)
  • Any urgency claim ("limited spots") unless the claim is literally true at the time of recording
  • Bible-verse-as-warrant for the methodology (the faith register is signature, not sermon)
  • Any "I've been blessed to lead" framing at the cohort-claim level (the audience-proof is observation, not performance)

End of VSL preview script.
