Why this shape

The consolidated home becomes the explicit four-tier ladder front door. The hero VSL pre-frames the algorithm methodology plus the literal redemption arc before any opt-in. The free chapter download is the lowest-friction lead-gen entry. The four-tier ladder gives every cohort (book reader, app user, mentorship applicant, corporate booker) a clear next-step. The TEDx + podcast + speaker capture funnel converts the highest-warmth audience segments into the owned-funnel. The recurring distribution layer compounds the funnel past install via LinkedIn corporate-keynote inbound and X long-form audience capture.

Tech Stack

  • Domain consolidation: terrancemcmahon.com canonical home. Re-Create app retained on its current infrastructure.
  • Site stack: existing site retained. Updates: hero VSL section, free chapter download capture, explicit four-tier ladder, companion landing pages.
  • Forms / quizzes: Typeform (low-friction, brand-aligned). Application form for the mentorship tier. Speaker-booking form for the enterprise tier.
  • Email / nurture: ConvertKit or Beehiiv recommended. ConvertKit if creator-native interface preferred, Beehiiv if growth + monetization features prioritized. Existing email infrastructure cloned cleanly if already in place.
  • VSL hosting: Vimeo (clean player, no recommended-video noise) or Wistia (better analytics) recommended over native YouTube embed.
  • Payment / membership: Re-Create app retains existing payment infrastructure. Stripe + application-based for the mentorship tier. Speaking / enterprise tier is invoice-based (B2B keynote standard).
  • Analytics: Plausible or Fathom (privacy-respecting, brand-aligned). Skip GA4 unless required for reporting compatibility.
  • Calendly: retained for higher-tier discovery calls only. Not the primary CTA on cold-traffic surfaces.
  • Book-to-funnel routing: QR codes on the back cover of future book printings plus a dedicated landing per book (e.g., book-1.terrancemcmahon.com or terrancemcmahon.com/book-1). Existing book printings link via free chapter download capture.

Risk Register

Risk 1 · Hero VSL recording delay

The Day-1 hero VSL is on the critical path. If recording slips, the consolidated home goes live without the VSL pre-frame and Phase Two ladder rollout proceeds anyway. Mitigation: record a Day-1 placeholder (lighter production, single-camera) that ships with the home page, replace with the produced version in Phase Three.

Risk 2 · First private mentorship cohort sign-up shortfall

If the first private mentorship cohort under-sells (target: 5-12 seats, stage-specific pricing confirmed at intake), the high-ticket lever does not produce the full Year-1 floor in the first 90 days. Mitigation: position the first cohort as intentionally small, build the second cohort's waitlist during the first cohort's run, second cohort sells from waitlist.

Risk 3 · Corporate-keynote segment slower to convert than B2C

LinkedIn corporate-keynote contracts are typically 30-90 day sales cycles, not 30-day cycles. Mitigation: plan for first corporate-keynote contract to close in the Day 60-90 window, not Day 30. Faith-business communities and Christian-business conferences may close on faster timelines (existing network effect from MassMutual seat + TEDx + bestseller credentials).

Risk 4 · The 21-month gap on the prior thread reads cold

The warm-reentry posture assumes the August 2024 thread is enough warm-context to lead with "made you something" framing. If Terrance has cycled through many similar inbound pitches in the gap, the warm-context lift may not produce. Mitigation: the cold-fallback version of the outreach is built and ready to deploy if the warm-reentry version doesn't surface engagement in the first 5-7 days.

Risk 5 · Tier ladder over-built relative to operator capacity

Four tiers is the right ladder shape for the audience footprint, but only if Terrance has the operator capacity to deliver the high-ticket tiers (private mentorship plus speaking) simultaneously. Mitigation: Phase One and Two ship the book and app tiers as the volume-friendly entry and recurring layers, mentorship and speaking tiers go live with application-only friction (no public pricing, no instant booking) so volume self-throttles to capacity.

Risk 6 · Stale testimonial inventory

The methodology has audience-proof at the cohort level (Re-Create app graduates, book reader feedback, TEDx viewers, keynote attendees) but named, attributable testimonials are not surfaced publicly during this audit pass. The landing page uses a placeholder block ([ Real testimonial attribution to be confirmed ]). Mitigation: testimonial swap is a Day-1 install task, the placeholder is a deliberate hold, not a deferral.

Risk 7 · Faith-register over-concentration on conversion surfaces

The "Bible, Business and AI" framing is a signature, not a sermon. If conversion copy concentrates the faith register too heavily, the methodology stops converting on methodology and starts converting only on scripture-as-warrant, which narrows the addressable audience past the Christian-business cohort. Mitigation: faith register stays in framing and bio, conversion copy stays methodology-led (see copy-strategy for the calibration).
